## What to Look for in a Cordless Vacuum in 2025

Before you click “buy,” a few key specs separate a great cordless vacuum from an average one. Here’s what to check.

### Suction Power and Performance
Suction is the heart of any vacuum. Measured in air watts (AW), top-tier cordless units now exceed 200AW – more than many plug-ins. Look for models that maintain strong suction even as the battery drains. If you have mixed flooring, choose a vacuum with a motorized brush roll that automatically adjusts speed for carpets and hard floors. In 2025, “smart” sensors that detect debris density and adjust power on the fly are common on high-end models.

### Battery Life and Charging
Runtime matters. Most cordless vacuums offer between 20 and 60 minutes on a single charge in standard mode, though high-power modes can cut that to under 10 minutes. The sweet spot for whole-home cleaning is around 40 minutes in auto/mid mode. Many premium models now ship with two swappable batteries, effectively doubling your cleaning time. Also check charging times – some fully charge in 3.5 hours, while others take 5+.

### Weight and Maneuverability
A cordless vacuum should feel comfortable in your hand. Most sticks weigh between 5 and 8 pounds. A well-balanced design and swivel steering make it easy to navigate under furniture. Features like a one-click removable handheld unit or a bendable wand (like Shark’s MultiFLEX) greatly improve reach and storage.

### Dustbin Capacity and Filtration
If you hate frequent emptying, aim for a dustbin of at least 0.5 liters. Bagless designs with “point-and-shoot” emptying reduce mess. For allergy sufferers, a fully sealed HEPA filtration system is a must – it traps 99.97% of particles as small as 0.3 microns.

### Smart Features and Attachments
In 2025, many vacuums come with LCD screens showing real-time battery, performance, and maintenance alerts. App connectivity, while not essential, can offer firmware updates and cleaning logs. Useful attachments include motorized pet hair brushes, flexible crevice tools, and wall-mounted charging docks that double as storage.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are cordless vacuums powerful enough to replace corded ones?**
For most households, yes. Top-tier cordless vacuums now deliver 200+ AW suction, which is enough for deep cleaning carpets and daily pickups. Corded vacuums may still have an edge for extremely thick carpets or heavy-duty garage work.

**How long do cordless vacuum batteries last?**
Lithium-ion batteries typically last 2–4 years with regular use, or about 500–800 charge cycles. Many manufacturers now offer replaceable batteries, extending the vacuum’s life considerably.

**Can I use a cordless vacuum on all floor types?**
Absolutely, but make sure the model has a motorized brush that can adjust to different surfaces. Some vacuum heads can manually or automatically switch between carpet and hard floor modes; using the right setting prevents scatter and maximizes pickup.

**What’s the benefit of a self-emptying dock?**
It reduces your exposure to dust when emptying the bin. Simply dock the vacuum and the station automatically sucks out the debris into a larger sealed bag, which you replace every month or two. It’s a game-changer for allergy sufferers.
